Solid State Drives

What do they do?

Solid state drives are a type of storage technology that used flash memory to allow rapid and low latency access to data. This is in contrast to solid state drives which read data from a physical platter.

Advantages of solid state storage

  • Faster data read
  • Faster data write
  • Less moving parts; withstand shock, vibration, heat, etc.
  • More compact
  • Easy to scale
  • Lower start-up time
  • Less noise
  • Can operate at any altitude
  • Lower power use

Disadvantages of Solid State Storage

  • More expensive
  • Unproven technology for long-term storage
  • Require power to preserve data long-term; data loss will occur after ~2 years without power
  • Hard to securely erase data

Components

  • NAND Flash chips.
  • Shroud
  • Host Interface
  • Battery
  • Catch/buffer
  • Memory Controller

Form Factors

  • SATA 2.5"
  • NVMe
  • PCIe
  • M.2
Other form factors are less common but also exist.

Sales

227 million units sold in 2017. Revenue was $585 million in 2008 - doubling from 2007. They are common in laptops as well as desktops - in the former they are used due to space constraints whilst in the latter they are used for their higher speeds.
