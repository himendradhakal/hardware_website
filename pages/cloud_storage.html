Cloud Storage

What is cloud storage?

 

Cloud storage is a model of computer data storage in which the digital data is stored in logical pools. The physical storage spans multiple servers and the physical environment is typically owned and managed by a hosting company. These cloud storage providers are responsible for keeping the data available and accessible, and the physical environment protected and running. People and organisations buy or lease storage capacity from the providers to store data.

 

Advantages

 

  • Business can cut their energy consumption by 70% by making them a more green business.
  • Cloud storage is easy to use and accessible.
  • Businesses can reduce annual operating costs by using cloud storage as cloud storage costs about 3 cents per GB to store data internally. Users can see additional cost savings because it does not require internal power to store information remotely.

 

Disadvantages

 

  • If you have no internet connection, you have no access to your data.
  • There are data security and privacy issues.
  • Backing up data to cloud storage may be slower than backing it up to local storage due to communication latency and the possibility of slow bandwidth

 

Costs

 

Google Cloud provides regional storage in London for $0.023 per GB per month. Amazon Web Services provides the first 50 TB for $0.024 per GB per month, then the next 450 TB for $0.023 per GB per month and over 500 TB for $0.022 per GB per month.

 

Industry Trends

 

The global market size was valued at $21,175 million in 2015, and is estimated to reach $97,415 million by 2022. Growth in demand for low cost data storage, increase in cloud adoption across several verticals, significant shift to hybrid cloud as primary deployment model and rise in concerns of data recovery and anticipated to drive the global cloud storage market. However, lack of IT infrastructure in underdeveloped regions in Africa, Middle East and Latin America and data security concerns over public cloud are expected to hinder the cloud storage market growth.

 

Cloud storage market in several European and Asian countries including Finland, Sweden, South Korea, Japan, and many others possess high market potential, and is projected to increase at a significant rate owing to strong government support and increase in cloud adoption among the SMEs in the region.

 

Key players in the cloud storage market are AWS, Microsoft, Google, IBM, HP, Oracle, EMC and Red Hat.

 

Advances in Cloud Storage

 

Cloud storage traditionally uses HDD storage but many hosting companies also provide SSD storage. This is because SSD is significantly faster and has more predictable performance than HDD. HDD throughput is much more limited than SSD throughput, so it is easier to reach the maximum throughput before CPU usage uses 100% using HDD storage. Individual row reads on HDD are very slow, which is only 5% of the read rows per second of SSD storage. However, HDD is still advantageous in some aspects. It is much cheaper, especially for large case uses such as a 10 TB. Many cloud hosting services also make the switch between HDD and SSD simple as you just need to export the data from the HDD and import it to the SSD.
