Printers

Lukas Dahmann & James Balenthiran

A bit about

A printer is a machine which is used to create a physical copy of a digital document.

Printers take longer to produce output from a computer than a computer.
A printed paper copy takes up much more space than a digital copy.

However the main advantage of printing data is you have a permanent copy.
This copy can be taken away from the computer or accessed when the computer is switched off.

How they work

Inkjet

The most common type of printer and the least expensive.
Spray ink in lines onto the paper from nozzles.
Different nozzles spray different colours – different signals from the computer change what is printed out.
Inkjets can produce a wide range of high quality colour pictures.
Useful for a small classroom printer or family printer.
However refilling the ink can be just as expensive as buying a new printer.

Laser

A laser printer uses fine black or coloured toner instead of ink.
It contains a drum or cylinder which is coated with static electricity.
The pattern of the static electricity is altered by a laser shined onto the drum.
The static electricity picks up the toner and makes it stick to the outside of the drum.
A piece of paper is then rolled past the drum and the toner drops off the drum printing the image to the paper.
Laser printers are typically much larger and more expensive.
They work a lot faster with the fastest printing 200 a minute – over 3 a second.
They typically only print black and white how coloured ones are available.

Inkjet vs Laser

Inkjet printers are generally cheaper to buy than laser printers.
Although, Laser printers are cheaper to print from with toner costing on average 3.1p per black page. Whereas inkjet printers cost over double the price at 6.6p.
Inkjet printers are also smaller and take up less space.
On the other hand laser printers print much faster.
Considering these points laser printers are better for commercial use as they are faster and as more pages will be printed – will be a better value for money.
Inkjet printers are more favourable for personal, family use as they are initially cheaper and shouldn’t need the ink replaced too often.

Ethics - Disposability

Laser printers have drum and toner cartridge which needs to be replaced or refilled whereas inkjet have lots of small parts so you can replace them individually upon a small fault.
As well as this, many companies are taking on take-back and remaking schemes to reduce the amount of waste that they produce.
