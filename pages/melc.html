MELC (Moral, Ethical, Law and Culture)

Laws

The Data Protection Act (2018 previously 1998)

The 2018 act makes processing of personal data subject to General Protection Regulation (GDPR). GDPR gives EU citizens extra protections and rights relating to their personal and private data online.

The 1998 act makes organisations storing personal data register with an Information Commissioner. Furthermore data must be processed with eight principles. These say the data must be:

  • fairly and lawfully processed
  • processed for limited purposes
  • adequate, relevant and not excessive
  • accurate
  • not kept longer than necessary
  • processed in accordance with the data subject's rights
  • secure
  • not transferred to other countries without adequate data protection

The Computer Misuse Act (1990)

Put in place to prevent hacking (data misuse). It contains three specific offences relating to computer usage:

  • Unauthorised access to computer programs or data
  • Unauthorised access with further criminal intent
  • Unauthorised modification of computer material

The Freedom of Information Act (2000)

This act extends the subject access rights of the Data Protection Act. The Act gives individuals access to both personal and non-personal data held by public authorities.

Regulation of Investigatory Powers Act (2000)

Clarifies the powers that the government have when investigating a crime. It gives law enforcement agencies the right to intercept communications and deciper encrypted data. It also allows employers to monitor the computer activity of their employees.

Copyright, Designs and Patents Act (1988)

This Act covers the copying, adapting and renting of materials - this includes content relating to computing e.g. web content, some types of data bases and comouter programs. Work produced on the internet also is covered by copyright.
